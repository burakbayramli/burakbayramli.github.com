Giant Beans (Gigandes)

Ingredients

  • 500g giant beans (dried)
  • 1 large onion
  • 2-3 stalks of celery (plus extra leaves for garnish)
  • 2 large carrots
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1 red pepper
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • Canned tomato (crushed or sauce)
  • Olive oil (extra virgin)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 veggie or chicken stock cube
  • Fresh rosemary
  • Lemon juice
  • Fresh herbs: parsley, spring onions
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Splash of wine
  • Feta cheese and dried oregano (for serving)

    • Description

  • Soak the dried beans in a bowl of fresh water with a pinch of salt for about 12 hours until they soften and expand.
  • Drain and wash the beans thoroughly. Place them in a pot, cover with fresh water, and boil for approximately one hour until they are slightly soft but still a bit crunchy.
  • While the beans boil, prepare the vegetables. Coarsely chop the onion, celery, carrots, garlic, and red pepper into large, rustic chunks.
  • In a pan, sauté the onions, celery, carrots, garlic, and red pepper in olive oil. Add the sugar, bay leaves, and cinnamon stick, and cook for about 10 minutes until caramelized.
  • Add the tomato paste to the vegetables and sauté briefly to enrich the flavor. Pour in the canned tomatoes, water, a splash of wine, the stock cube, salt, pepper, rosemary, and a squeeze of lemon.
  • Drain the boiled beans and place them into a large baking dish. Pour the vegetable and tomato sauce mixture over the beans and mix well.
  • Top the mixture with chopped celery leaves, spring onions, and parsley.
  • Cover the dish tightly with aluminum foil. Bake in a preheated oven at 180°C (fan setting) for 1 hour and 15 minutes.
  • Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes to allow a crust to form on the top.
  • Serve warm, topped with fresh parsley, a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil, feta cheese, and a sprinkle of oregano.
